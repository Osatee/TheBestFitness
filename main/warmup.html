How important is a "Warm-up"


A warm-up involves gentle movements to increase your body temperature. It is stretching to prepare the muscles and stimulates the heart rate to be higher. Increase the potential for exercise or playing more sports.

Warm up steps

In principle A human cell, a warm-blooded animal, has a body temperature of about 37 °C, plus or minus 0.5 °C, which is between 36.5 and 37.5 °C. The body temperature should be at 38 degrees, that's why it needs to be warmed up to allow our bodies to rise. Be ready for exercise or use the muscles heavier fully, which is the most efficient Warm up. It will take about 20 minutes.

Cooldown steps

When the exercise has reached its goal and it's time to stop. An important thing not to be overlooked is to slowly return the body to its normal pre-exercise state by gradually lowering the heart rate. Because while exercising, the heart will work hard. The body will be stressed. the muscles are destroyed And waste products are produced in various cells. Cool down is the best way to maintain the level. And get rid of the waste that the body creates while exercising. It should take about 5-10 minutes to stretch the muscles. By doing it in a way that stretches the muscles by holding each position for 10-15 seconds, stopping the exercise abruptly without Cool down will cause fainting. Dizziness or loss of consciousness As for the muscles, there will be contractions and contractions. This can cause muscle stiffness and pain in the following days.

Stretching Excercises steps

Stretching is good and necessary. It will increase the flexibility and relaxation of the muscles. The muscles are not too stressed or too tight. Therefore, both before and after exercise. So we should always stretch our muscles. by doing it together with warm-ups and cool-downs.
